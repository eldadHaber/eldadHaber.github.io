About

I am a Professor at UBC specializing in computational inverse problems, machine learning, and geosciences. My work focuses on the development of stable numerical methods for deep learning, often motivated by Partial Differential Equations (PDEs) and dynamical systems.

I was named a SIAM Fellow in 2022 and hold a U.S. Department of Energy Career Award. Beyond academia, I have co-founded several technology startups, including Computational Geosciences Inc. and Xtract.ai.

Research Interests

  • Machine Learning: Reversible architectures, PDE-GCNs, and stable DNNs.
  • Geophysics: 3D electromagnetic problems, resistivity inversion, and mineral mapping.
  • Inverse Problems: Large-scale optimization and parameter estimation.

Software & Projects

  • CompGeo: Public repositories for computational geosciences.
  • InvertibleNetworks.jl: Scalable normalizing flows (collaborative).
  • TANGO: Graph Neural Dynamics via learned energy flows.
